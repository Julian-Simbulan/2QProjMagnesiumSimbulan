Hostile Mobs

Hostiles are aggressive creatures that actively attack the player on sight. They are often dangerous and require weapons to confront them.

Blaze

Blazes are flying multiple-limbed mobs found in nether fortresses. Blazes sink to the ground when not active, are immune to lava and fire, and get hurt by water. They attack players by setting themselves on fire, then shooting fireballs in bursts of 3 towards a player that they can directly see, setting the target on fire upon a successful hit. They can alert nearby blazes when a blaze is hurt by a player or mob.

Spawn Conditions: light level of 11- in nether fortresses; blaze spawners

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage:
6 hp (3 x ) from melee
5 hp (2.5 x ) from fireball
1 hp (0.5 x ) per 1.25 sec for 5 sec from fire

XP Drop: 10 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

blaze rod 0-1 when killed

Fun Fact:
Blazes do not actually have a core to their body; they simply compose of a head and floating rods.

Creeper

Creepers are common deadly mobs that spawn in the dark. They are afraid of felines, and are best known for their iconic appearance. They attack players by approaching them, halting to hiss for 1.5 seconds, then exploding, destroying nearby blocks, greaty damaging their target and killing themselves in the process. They are also capable of attacks from above, and can turn into charged creepers when struck by lightning. Their detonation can be avoided by killing them or by going far away from the creepers.

Spawn Conditions: on solid blocks with light level of 7-

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage:
43 hp (21.5 x ) at most from regular creeper
85 hp (42.5 x ) at most from charged creeper

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

gunpowder 0-2 when killed
random music disc (not pigstep) 1 when killed by skeleton/stray
creeper head 1 when killed by charged creeper

Fun Fact:
Creepers were born from a coding bug when Notch was creating the model for the pig.

Drowned

Drowned are underwater undead humanoid mobs found in dark waters. They stay in water during the day, swimming around until a player steps into the water, due to them burning up in daylight. At night, they can step out of the water and attack turtle eggs, baby turtles, villagers, wandering traders, iron golems and players. They attack nearby targets through melee combat, and if they have a trident, they will also use ranged attacks by throwing tridents at their target.

Spawn Conditions: underwater with light level 7- and below y=58 in ocean biomes; underwater with light level 7- in rivers; near ocean ruins; when a zombie drowns

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage:
3 hp (1.5 x ) from melee
8 hp (4 x ) from ranged

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP for every equipment worn when killed as adult; 12 XP when killed as baby

Loot Drops:

rotten flesh 0-2 when killed
nautilus shell 1 when killed with this on their hand (3% chance)
gold ingot 5% chance when killed
trident 8.5% chance when killed
equipment worn 100% chance when killed

Fun Fact:
Drowned have sufficient skill to hit players in boats at maximum speed.

Elder Guardian

Elder guardians are large water-based mobs found inside ocean monuments. They barely move, target nearby players and squid, and give nearby players Mining Fatigue III. They attack by firing a laser from their eyes to their target, taking a while to charge before shooting, and by extending spikes surrounding their body if their target is close enough.

Spawn Conditions: 3 inside every ocean monument

Health: 80 hp (40 x )

Damage:
8 hp (4 x ) from laser
2 hp (1 x ) from spikes

XP Drop: 10 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

prismarine shard 0-2 when killed
wet sponge 1 when killed
raw cod 50% chance when killed w/o fire
cooked cod 50% chance when killed w/ fire
prismarine crystals 66.7% chance when killed and did not drop raw/cooked cod
random fish 2.5% chance when killed (does not affect prismarine crystal drop rates)

Fun Fact:
Elder guardians jumpscare players when they inflict mining fatigue on the latter. This wasn't intentional, and was supposed to be just a visual cue.

Endermite

Endermite are small annoying mobs that spawn when the player uses an ender pearl. They aggravate nearby endermen, attack nearby players, and despawn within 2 minutes. They attack by hitting their target's legs.

Spawn Conditions: 5% chance when an ender pearl lands

Health: 8 hp (4 x )

Damage: 2 hp (1 x )

XP Drop: 3 XP when killed

Loot Drops: none

Fun Fact:
Endermite are the smallest hostile mobs in the game.

Evoker

Evokers are spell-casting illagers that spawn in woodland mansions and raids. They target villagers, iron golems and players, and avoid players when not ready. They attack using 2 methods: fang attacks and vexes. When using fang attacks, they would summon fangs in a pattern of either 2 circles of 13 fangs around themselves, or a straight line of 16 fangs towards their target. When using vexes, they would summon 3 vexes at a time until the number of vexes nearby is greater than 7.

Spawn Conditions: in woodland mansions; during raids

Health: 24 hp (12 x )

Damage: 6 hp (3 x )

XP Drop: 10 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

totem of undying 1 when killed
emerald 0-1 when killed
ominous banner 1 when killed w/ this item equipped

Fun Fact:
Evokers change the color of nearby blue-dyed sheep to red. This is a reference to priests in "Age of Empires", who can convert nearby enemies into friendlies.

Ghast

Ghasts are giant floating mobs found in the Nether. They float around and cry, and target nearby players that are in their direct line of sight. They attack by shooting fireballs from their mouths, which deals damage to blocks and players on impact, and set the surrounding impact area on fire.

Spawn Conditions: on solid blocks in basalt deltas, nether wastes and soul sand valley biomes

Health: 10 hp (5 x )

Damage:
6 hp (3 x ) from direct impact
12 hp (6 x ) at most from explosion

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

ghast tear 0-1 when killed
gunpowder 0-2 when killed

Fun Fact:
The sounds that ghasts make are actually the sounds made by the cat of one of the OST producers of Minecraft, C418.

Guardian

Guardians are water-based one-eyed mobs found near ocean monuments. They swim around spontaneously, stare at nearby players, and target nearby players and squid. They attack by firing a laser from their eyes to their target, taking a while to charge before shooting, and by extending spikes surrounding their body if their target is close enough.

Spawn Conditions: underwater between y=39 to y=61 near ocean monuments

Health: 30 hp (15 x )

Damage:
6 hp (3 x ) from laser
2 hp (1 x ) from spikes

XP Drop: 10 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

prismarine shard 0-2 when killed
raw cod 40% chance when killed w/o fire
cooked cod 40% chance when killed w/ fire
prismarine crystals 66.7% chance when killed and did not drop raw/cooked cod
random fish 2.5% chance when killed (does not affect prismarine crystal drop rates)

Fun Fact:
In order for guardians to have the dynamic eye, the eye is coded as the "head" of the mob model. The head of many mob models follow the player around when they are close enough.

Hoglin

Hoglins are giant pig-based mobs found in the Nether. They are afraid of warped fungi, nether portals and respawn anchors, and can be bred using crimson fungi. They attack players on sight and piglins that attacked them by flinging their target using their head, and alerting nearby hoglins.

Spawn Conditions: in crimson forest biomes in herds of 3-4; inside hoglin stables in bastion remnants

Health: 40 hp (20 x )

Damage: 0.5 hp (0.25 x ) - 8 hp (4 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

leather 0-2 when killed
raw porkchop 2-4 when killed w/o fire
cooked porkchop 2-4 when killed w/ fire

Fun Fact:
Hoglins are the only breedable hostile mobs.

Husk

Husks are undead humanoid mobs found in dark places in deserts. Husks do not burn in daylight, sometimes wear armor, and attack turtle eggs, baby turtles, villagers, wandering traders, iron golems and players. They attack nearby targets through melee combat, applying Hunger to their target in the process.

Spawn Conditions: light level of 7- in desert biome variants in groups of 4, 5% chance to spawn as a chicken jockey

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage: 3 hp (1.5 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP for every equipment worn when killed as adult; 12 XP when killed as baby

Loot Drops:

rotten flesh 0-2 when killed
iron ingot, carrot, or potato 2.5% chance for one of the three to drop when killed
naturally-spawned equipment worn 8.5% chance when killed
picked-up equipment worn 100% chance when killed

Fun Fact:
Husks are slightly taller than zombies and drowned.

Magma Cube

Magma cubes are varying-sized cubes found in the Nether. They are immune to lava and fire, randomly hop around, and target nearby iron golems and players. They attack by randomly jumping at their target, hurting the latter in the process. When killed, they split into 2-4 smaller versions of themselves.

Spawn Conditions: in nether waste biomes in groups of up to 4; in nether fortresses in groups of 4; in basalt delta biomes in groups of 2-5; magma cube spawners in bastion remnants

Phantom

Phantoms are stingray-based flying mobs that haunt players when they haven't slept in a while. They burn in daylight, avoid nearby cats, and circle at a certain altitude when idle. They attack by swooping down to hurt their target then retreating to their original altitude.

Spawn Conditions: when the player hasn't slept for 3+ in-game days (1+ hour), at nighttime or thunderstorms in the Overworld

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage: 2 hp (1 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

phantom membrane 0-2 when killed

Fun Fact:
Phantoms were originally red, but was then scrapped because it gave the misconception that they are Nether mobs.

Pillager

Pillagers are crossbow-wielding illagers that spawn in patrols, near outposts, and during raids. They attack villagers, wandering traders, iron golems and players. When in patrols, they randomly wander in a group, targeting valid targets. Near pillager outposts, they attack targets that get to close to the outpost. During raids, they actively pathfind to villages and kill targets they encounter, and celebrate when they do a successful raid. They attack by using their crossbows to shoot their target at range.

Spawn Conditions: in patrols in groups of 5; near outposts; during raids

Health: 24 hp (12 x )

Damage: 4 hp (2 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP for every equipment worn when killed

Loot Drops:

crossbow 8.5% chance when killed
ominous banner 1 when killed w/ this item equipped

Fun Fact:
They are the only illager that do not fold their arms.

Ravager

Ravagers are large four-legged beasts that accompany illagers during raids. They can break leaves and crops, deal knockback, and target villagers, wandering traders, iron golems and players. They attack by ramming their target with their head, dealing damage and knockback. They can be blocked with a shield, which has a chance to stun them. However, they will retaliate by roaring, dealing damage and knockback to nearby mobs.

Spawn Conditions: part of raids from wave 3 onwards

Health: 100 hp (50 x )

Damage:
12 hp (6 x ) from melee
6 hp (3 x ) from roar

XP Drop: 20 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

saddle 1 when killed

Fun Fact:
Ravagers are inspired by Manticores, a creature with the head of a human, the body of a lion and a tail similar to porcupine quills.

Shulker

Shulkers are shelled block-shaped mobs found in the End. They stay stationary, attaching themselves to a block and blending in with their surroundings, and target nearby players. When they have low health or their anchor block is compromised, they teleport to a safer location and anchor themselves to a new block. They attack by shooting a guided projectile that targets nearby players, which will apply Levitation to their target upon contact.

Spawn Conditions: in end cities

Health: 30 hp (15 x )

Damage: 4 hp (2 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

shulker shell 50% chance when killed

Fun Fact:
Shulkers were originally suppose to have active camouflage that change based on the surrounding blocks, but was scrapped because of the difficulty in coding it.

Silverfish

Silverfish are small annoying mobs found inside infested blocks. They often hide inside stone-based blocks until they have been disturbed or alerted. They attack players that broke their block or have come too close, hitting their target's legs and alerting nearby silverfish to come out of their blocks.

Spawn Conditions: from infested blocks in strongholds, igloo basements and mansions; from infested blocks in mountain biomes; silverfish spawners in strongholds

Health: 8 hp (4 x )

Damage: 1 hp (0.5 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops: none

Fun Fact:
A version of the silverfish known as "redstone bug" appeared in "Minecraft 2.0", an April Fool's Joke edition of the game.

Skeleton

Skeletons are common bow-wielding skeleton-based mobs found in dark places. They burn in daylight, sometimes wear armor, flee from wolves, and target players they see and mobs that hurt them. They attack by shooting arrows from their bow at range, strafing and dodging incoming attacks while doing so.

Spawn Conditions: on solid blocks with light level of 7-; in nether fortresses; skeleton spawners in dungeons

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage:
2.5 hp (1.25 x ) from melee
2 hp (1 x ) - 5 hp (2.5 x ) from range

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP if it has equipment worn when killed

Loot Drops:

bone 0-2 when killed
arrow 0-2 when killed
naturally-spawned equipment worn 8.5% chance when killed
picked-up equipment worn 100% chance when killed
skeleton skull 1 when killed by charged creeper

Fun Fact:
89% of skeletons hold their bow with their right hand. This indicates that most skeletons are left eye dominant.

Slime

Slimes are varying-sized cubes that spawn in swamps and slime chunks. They randomly hop around and wander, searching for targets which are iron golems and players. They attack by randomly jumping at their target, hurting the latter in the process. When killed, they split into 2-4 smaller versions of themselves.

Spawn Conditions: on any solid block between y=50 to y=70 and light level of 7- in swamp biomes; below y=40 in slime chunks

Stray

Strays are bow-wielding skeleton-based mobs found in cold biomes. They burn in daylight, sometimes wear armor, flee from wolves, and target players they see and mobs that hurt them. They attack by shooting arrows that inflict Slowness to their target from their bow at range, strafing and dodging incoming attacks.

Spawn Conditions: light level of 7- in snowy tundra, snowy mountain, ice spikes and frozen river biomes.

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage:
2.5 hp (1.25 x ) from melee
2 hp (1 x ) - 5 hp (2.5 x ) from range

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP if it has equipment worn when killed

Loot Drops:

bone 0-2 when killed
arrow 0-2 when killed
arrow of slowness 0-1 when killed
naturally-spawned equipment worn 8.5% chance when killed
picked-up equipment worn 100% chance when killed

Fun Fact:
The origins of stray come from a concept made by Jason Major during a game jam.

Vex

Vexes are small deadly flying mobs that are part of evoker's attacks. They can fly and phase through blocks, target villagers, wandering traders, iron golems and players, and begin to die after at most 2 minutes. When attacking, they turn red and lunge towards their target. Hurting a vex will cause all nearby vexes to target the player.

Spawn Conditions: summoned by evokers

Health: 14 hp (7 x )

Damage: 9 hp (4.5 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops: none

Fun Fact:
Vexes are not considered undead mobs even though they act and look like ghosts.

Vindicator

Vindicators are axe-wielding illagers that spawn in woodland mansions and raids. They target villagers, wandering traders, iron golems and players. During raids, they sometimes spawn riding a ravager, actively pathfind to villages and kill targets they encounter, and celebrate when they do a successful raid. They attack by sprinting towards their target with their axe out, disabling shields and dealing massive damage. Hurting a vindicator will cause all nearby vindicators to target the player.

Spawn Conditions: in woodland mansions; during raids

Health: 24 hp (12 x )

Damage:
13 hp (6.5 x ) when armed
5 hp (2.5 x ) when unarmed

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP for every equipment worn when killed

Loot Drops:

emeralds 0-1 when killed
iron axe 8.5% chance when killed
ominous banner 1 when killed w/ this item equipped

Fun Fact:
Naming a vindicator "Johnny" will cause it to become aggressive towards most mobs. This is a reference to "The Shining".

Witch

Witches are uncommon humanoid mobs that uses potions for offense and defense. They are resistant to harming and poison potions, evoker fangs and thorns. During raids, they support nearby pillagers by throwing splash potions of regeneration, and celebrate when they do a successful raid. They attack players using splash potions of slowness, poison, weakness and harming, and defend themselves with potions of water breathing, fire resistance, healing, and swiftness.

Spawn Conditions: on solid blocks with light level of 7-; in swamp huts; when a villager is struck by lightning; during raids

Health: 26 hp (13 x )

Damage:
6 hp (3 x ) from splash potion of harming
1 hp (0.5 x ) per 1.25 sec for at most 45 sec from splash potion of poison

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

potion of healing, fire resistance, swiftness or water breathing 8.5% chance when killed

  up to 6 of the following items:

glass bottle 12.5% chance of 0-2 when killed
glowstone dust 12.5% chance of 0-2 when killed
gunpowder 12.5% chance of 0-2 when killed
redstone 12.5% chance of 0-2 when killed
spider eye 12.5% chance of 0-2 when killed
glass bottle 12.5% chance of 0-2 when killed
glass bottle 12.5% chance of 0-2 when killed

Fun Fact:
Witches are based on "Baba Yaga", a Slavic folklore about a witch living in a hut that is standing on chicken legs.

Wither Skeleton

Wither skeletons are sword-wielding skeleton-based mobs found in nether fortresses. They are immune to fire and lava, flee from wolves, and target iron golems, baby turtles, piglins and players. They attack by sprinting towards their target and swinging their sword, dealing normal damage and wither damage.

Spawn Conditions: on solid blocks with light level of 7- in nether fortresses in groups of 4

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage: 8 hp (4 x ) + 1 hp (0.5 x ) per 2 sec for 10 sec

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP for every equipment worn when killed

Loot Drops:

bone 0-2 when killed
coal 33.3% chance when killed
stone sword 8.5% chance when killed
wither skeleton skull 2.5% chance when killed or 100% chance when killed by charged creeper
picked-up equipment worn 100% chance when killed

Fun Fact:
Wither skeletons are larger than their other skeleton counterparts, and cannot fit through 2-high holes.

Zoglin

Zoglins are zombified counterparts of hoglins. They are immune to fire and lava damage, and do not have the same weaknesses as their living counterpart. They are highly aggressive, targeting any entity nearby that is not a creeper nor a zoglin. They attack by flinging their target using their head.

Spawn Conditions: when a hoglin is outside the Nether dimension for more than 15 seconds

Health: 40 hp (20 x )

Damage: 0.5 hp (0.25 x ) - 8 hp (4 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP when killed

Loot Drops:

rotten flesh 1-3 when killed

Fun Fact:
The tusks of zoglins have slightly different textures, causing them to have separate textures for each tusk.

Zombie

Zombies are common undead humanoid mobs that spawn in the dark. They burn in daylight, sometimes wear armor, have farther detection range than other hostiles, can break down doors, and attack turtle eggs, baby turtles, villagers, wandering traders, iron golems and players. They attack nearby targets through melee combat, and when a zombie is hurt by the player, nearby zombies are alerted to target the player.

Spawn Conditions: on solid blocks with light level of 7- in groups of 4; when a husk drowns; during zombie sieges; zombie spawners in dungeons

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage: 3 hp (1.5 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP for every equipment worn when killed as adult; 12 XP + 1-3 XP for every equipment worn when killed as baby

Loot Drops:

rotten flesh 0-2 when killed
iron ingot, carrot, or potato 2.5% chance for one of the three to drop when killed
naturally-spawned equipment worn 8.5% chance when killed
picked-up equipment worn 100% chance when killed

Fun Fact:
Zombies are one of the oldest hostile mobs that have not been removed.

Zombie Villager

Zombie villagers are zombified counterparts of villagers. They behave mostly the same with normal zombies, with the exception that they cannot be converted to drowned. They can be cured using a splash potion of weakness and a normal golden apple, which will convert them back to normal villagers. These converted villagers will offer large discounts to the player that cured them.

Spawn Conditions: on solid blocks with light level of 7-; in igloo basements; 50% chance when a villager is killed by a zombified mob

Health: 20 hp (10 x )

Damage: 3 hp (1.5 x )

XP Drop: 5 XP + 1-3 XP for every equipment worn when killed as adult; 12 XP when killed as baby

Loot Drops:

rotten flesh 0-2 when killed
iron ingot, carrot, or potato 2.5% chance for one of the three to drop when killed
naturally-spawned equipment worn 8.5% chance when killed
picked-up equipment worn 100% chance when killed

Fun Fact:
The rarest mob that can spawn in the game is "a left-handed, leader baby [zombie] villager nitwit chicken jockey that can pick up items, can't break doors, has full enchanted diamond gear, with level 20, an enchanted iron sword with level 16, on a slime chunk desert during Halloween, under specific conditions", which has an 4.3797e-75% chance to spawn on the worst-case scenario.
